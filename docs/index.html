Why it exists

Clipboard managers remember the last few copies.
This remembers everything you ever cared about.

If you copied it, you cared about it — and you might need it again. Most clipboard tools optimize for recent recall: a scrollable list of your last N copies. They can't answer "that auth thing I pasted last Tuesday." This turns your clipboard into a durable, searchable, programmable knowledge layer that lives entirely on your machine.

☾ Mother · Moon

The Watcher

Runs the capture loop. Records every snippet with its timestamp, frontmost app, and window title. Deduplicates, prunes to your cap, ingests files — ever-listening, ever-recording.

☉ Father · Sun

The Retriever

Runs search, recent, export, and stats. Queried on demand, always ready with answers — by keyword, by meaning, or by recency. The active, solar half of the system.

Capabilities

Built to find what you copied.

🔎

Full-text search

SQLite FTS5 over every clip you've ever captured. Instant keyword matches across months of history.

🧠

Semantic search

Find by meaning, not just words. "OAuth credential rotation" matches "auth token refresh" even with zero overlapping words. Pro

🔒

Local-only by design

Everything lives in ~/.my-father-mother/mfm.db. No cloud, no telemetry, no account. Ever.

🛡️

Secret-aware

Detects and skips AWS keys, GitHub PATs, private keys, and Slack tokens before they're ever stored.

🏷️

Tags & pins

Annotate, tag, and pin the clips that matter so they survive eviction and stay one query away.

📝

Auto-summarization

Long clips get a one-line summary so your history stays scannable at a glance. Pro

🔌

MCP bridge

Expose your clipboard memory to AI coding tools over the Model Context Protocol. API-first, scriptable.

⚡

Zero dependencies

One Python file, stdlib only. Clone, init, and you're running. No build step, no venv juggling.

zsh — my--father-mother
# Mother starts watching your clipboard
$ python3 main.py watch --cap 5000

# Father finds it again — by keyword
$ python3 main.py search "docker env"

# ...or by meaning (Pro)
$ python3 main.py semantic-search "api authentication"
→ matched "OAuth token refresh flow" copied 6 days ago
